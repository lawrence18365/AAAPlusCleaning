Castle Hill, New South Wales: A Family-Friendly Haven

Located in Sydney's north-western region, approximately 28.3 kilometers from the CBD, Castle Hill stands as a testament to suburban excellence. This highly-rated suburb in the Blacktown City local government area offers residents a perfect blend of community spirit and modern conveniences, earning an impressive 9.4/10 livability rating.

The Roots of Castle Hill: A Historical Perspective

Named after Castle Hill Manor House in Hertfordshire, England, by early settler Matthew Pearce, the suburb was officially recognized in 1987. The area's transformation into a residential community began in the 1970s, building a foundation for what would become one of Sydney's most sought-after neighborhoods.

Education: Foundations for the Future

Castle Hill provides quality education through:

  • Vardys Road Primary School (est. 1959)
  • Castle Hill Public School (est. 1981)
  • Easy access to high schools in Baulkham Hills and Blacktown

Community Demographics and Housing

With a population of 9,354 (2021 census), Castle Hill boasts a diverse community where 69.8% are Australian-born. The suburb features predominantly separate houses, with families enjoying home ownership and reasonable housing costs, including median weekly rentals of $480.
